README

The Monty Hall problem is a probability-based puzzle loosely based on the American television game show Let's Make a Deal and named after its original host, Monty Hall. The rules of the game are simple, there are three doors behind one of which there is a Car. You have to choose a door, After you choose a door the host who knows what's behind each door opens one of the other two doors which does not have a car. Now you are given a choice, whether you stick to your original choice or switch to the remaining door? What do you think will give you a mathematical advantage? Now that there are two doors, and behind one of which there is a car, Do you think it's 50-50? Well, there are plenty of resources available on the correct answer and the intuition for it. Try playing this game with the two strategies and see if you could figure out what is happening.
